# Vision Statement

## The Vision: A Campus of Hospitality

The University of Colorado Boulder is more than a world-class research institution; it is a physical landscape that must speak with clarity to those who enter it. For the first-time visitor, the campus is often an overwhelming tapestry of buff sandstone and intricate corridors. Our new Wayfinding Standards Manual is founded on the belief that navigation is the first act of hospitality. By designing for the most vulnerable user—the guest arriving for an orientation, the parent seeking a specific hall, or the patient visiting a campus clinic—we cultivate a sense of security and goodwill that reinforces our institutional excellence. This system is a long-term investment in the "Calm Campus" initiative, a commitment to reducing the visual noise and environmental anxiety that so often characterizes large-scale institutional environments.

A legible campus is one where the architecture and the signage work in a state of navigational harmony. We do not view wayfinding as a series of bandages applied to a confusing site; rather, we treat it as an integrated layer of the built environment that reveals the university’s logic in sequence. This approach requires an "earned" intuition, where neighborhood names and boundaries resonate with the physical features—the topography, the trees, and the water—that a visitor can immediately perceive. By anchoring our nomenclature in the landforms of the Boulder landscape, we ensure that the system remains valid through decades of academic shifts and physical growth. Our vision is to transform the campus from a fragmented collection of buildings into a single, connected image that fosters confidence and exploration.
