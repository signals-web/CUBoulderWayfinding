# CU Boulder Philosophy

## The Graduated Logic: Navigating the Campus

To manage cognitive load effectively, the university utilizes a "Graduated Scale" of information density. This philosophy moves away from the "all-inclusive" map in favor of a sequence of nesting steps that mirror the user’s journey. At the campus perimeter and primary arrival gateways, we prioritize the "Campus-Wide Scale." At these entry points, our maps and markers do not attempt to identify every individual building. Instead, they prioritize orientation, emphasizing major destinations, distinct districts, and the primary open spaces that define the university’s character. By focusing on neighborhoods rather than specific room numbers at the perimeter, we help the visitor establish a broad mental model of the campus before they take their first step into the interior.

As the user moves from the perimeter toward their destination, the system "zooms in" to the "Local Area Scale." Only at these internal decision points does the informational density increase, introducing specific building names and pedestrian paths relevant to that immediate neighborhood. This neighborhood logic is governed by the "Philosophy of Scale," which ensures that the user is never overwhelmed by irrelevant data. We have organized the landscape into intuitive zones like "The Quad," "Creekside," and "The Ridge"—names that are earned because they reflect the physical reality of the site. By aligning these neighborhood "edges" with the primary corridors people already use intuitively, we create a navigable landscape that feels natural rather than manufactured.
